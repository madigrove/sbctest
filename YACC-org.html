    YAC

    The Young Authors Conference (lovingly, YAC) is a one-day field trip that takes place at the Bethel University main campus. Students attend a keynote address and interactive writing sessions led by local authors and writing professionals.

    School groups can select availability for March 20, 2020 or May 26, 27, 28, or 29, 2020.


    At the Young Authors Conference, students...

    • Are challenged and encouraged to be better writers.
    • Leave with new strategies, ideas, and skills to become a better writer.
    • Learn that strong writing skills are important in life.


    For more information about the Young Authors Conference and how your students can attend, contact Colleen Feller at 612-638-1511.

    2020 Theme & Keynotes

    This year we are proud to celebrate the 30th anniversary of the Young Authors Conference!


    The 2020 Young Authors Conference theme is Words Have Power. Words and stories hold tremendous power: they spark imagination, inspire confidence, help heal, honor family, show gratitude, demonstrate kindness, and share truth.

    Geoff Herbach


    Geoff Herbach is the author of 10 novels, including those in the Stupid Fast trilogy, Fat Boy vs. The Cheerleaders, and Hooper. His new book, Cracking the Bell, hit the shelves in September of 2019. Geoff's accolades include the Cybils Award for best YA fiction and the Minnesota Book Award. In the past, Geoff wrote and performed comedy and traveled around the country telling weird stories in rock clubs. Geoff teaches writing at Minnesota State, Mankato and loves presenting at YAC.

    Kevin Kling


    Kevin Kling, renowned Minnesota storyteller, has written plays for children that have premiered at the Children's Theatre Company in Minneapolis and Seattle Children's Theater. His one-person shows and full cast plays have been produced at regional theaters such as The Guthrie and The Kennedy Center. Kevin has written 5 books and appears often on PBS and Minnesota Public Radio. Kevin is most proud of his work as a teacher, playwright and performer with Interact Center, a company that creates art in the spirit of radical inclusion.


    At the heart of our stories, we write about how families, friends and communities bolster us during challenging experiences, and these are the stories that feather our nest. But as writers, the creative force comes from what makes each of us different. We often learn the greatest truths when we are able to distill and value our unique perspective.


    Impact Report

    94% of students gave the Young Authors Conference a 4 or 5, out of 5.
    72% of students had never attended the Young Authors Conference and 95% of students that attended want to come back next year.

    98% of students said the Young Authors Conference gave them new strategies, ideas, and skills to become a better writer.
    95% of students left the Young Authors Conference understanding that strong writing skills are important in life.
